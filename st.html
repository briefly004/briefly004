5G Technology

In telecommunications, 5G is fifth generation technology for cellular networks .
5G is widely believed to be faster , smarter , & more efficient than the current 4G network.
Speed provided by 5G is 100 gigabits per second (100 times faster than 4G).
It will provide faster downloads speed , low latency & more capacity and connectivity .
5G has been contributed by several companies. A lot of countries are now having this technology.
To use 5G one need a 5G phone .
In India, 5G spectrum sale was also going to happen but it has postponed to 2021.
5G Spectrum is actually a frequency range of 3.3GHz to 3.6GHz.

Advantages :- 1-High resolution and bi-directional large bandwidth shaping.
2-Technology to gather all networks on one platform.
3-More effective and efficient.
4-Technology to facilitate subscriber supervision tools for the quick action.
5-Most likely, will provide a huge broadcasting data (in Gigabit), which will support more than 60,000 connections.
6-Easily manageable with the previous generations.
7-Technological sound to support heterogeneous services (including private network).
8-Possible to provide uniform, uninterrupted, and consistent connectivity across the world.
Disdvantages :- 1-Many of the old devices would not be competent to 5G, hence, all of them need to be replaced with new one — expensive deal.
2-Developing infrastructure needs high cost.
3-Security and privacy issue yet to be solved.
4-The radiation from 5G mobile phones might be harmful for animals and birds too; for example, high-frequency electromagnetic waves are said to alter insects’ body temperatures and impair their ability to orient themselves.
