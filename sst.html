INDIAN CULTURE
INDIA A LAND OF UNITY IN DIVERSITY

" Unity in diversity"refers to state of togetherness.
The history of ancient india is intresting because india proved to be a melting pot of numerous races.
the Pre-aryans ,the indo -aryans ,the greeks, the scythians, the hunas , the turks ,etc. made india their home each group contributed its culture to the making of the indian culture.
All theswe people mixed in such a inseperately manner with one another that it is very difficult that at present none of them can be indefied in their present form.
Different culture mingled with one another through the ages.Since ancient india has been the land of several religion. Ancient india witnessed the birth of Hinduism,jainism and buddhism.All these cultures and religions inermingled with one another. Therefore our country shows a deep underlying root of unity inspite of great diverity.
From the Indus valley civilisation to the India today,Hinduism,Islam,Buddhism,Jainism,Sikhism, Christianity and many other religion peacefully coexist.
Unity in diversity is one of the most powerful characteristics.
India has proved throughout the years and years of history that diversity does not pull people apart.
It brings them closer together. The spirit of "unity in diversity" also put into our National Anthem by the phrase " Punjab,Sindh,Gujarat,Maratha,Dravida,Utkal-Banga"
The Unity was also recognised by foreigners. they first came into contact with the people living on the side of Sindu or Indus, and so they named the whole country after this river.
The word "Hind" is derived from the term "Sindhu" and in course of time the country came to known as "India" in greek and "Hind" in persian and arabic langauges . The Indian culture and values and ideas were expressed in different forms, the substance remained the same throughout the country.
Hence India has emerged a muli-Religion and multi-Cultural society.
However the underlying unity and integrity and plural character of india society remains the real strength for the development of the country. by Aeshwarya Pratap Singh
